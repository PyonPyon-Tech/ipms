Artists

Artists are the driving creative force behind music. Hereinafter I shall list some of my favourite artists that I'd like to highlight, that have had a significant influence in my life.

The Beach Boys

The Beach Boys are an iconic American rock band that originated in California in the early 1960s. Their signature harmonies and surf-rock sound have become synonymous with the laid-back, carefree spirit of the West Coast. Led by the Wilson brothers, Brian, Dennis, and Carl, along with their cousin Mike Love and friend Al Jardine, the Beach Boys rose to fame with hits like "Surfin' USA," "Good Vibrations," and "California Girls." Their music captures the essence of summer and the joys of youth, with lyrics that celebrate love, friendship, and the natural beauty of the beach. The Beach Boys have left an indelible mark on American music and continue to be beloved by fans all over the world.

The Velvet Underground

The Velvet Underground is a legendary American rock band that emerged in the mid-1960s, with a sound that defied convention and pushed the boundaries of what was considered acceptable in popular music. Fronted by the enigmatic Lou Reed and featuring John Cale, Sterling Morrison, and Maureen Tucker, the Velvet Underground's music was marked by its raw, experimental sound and often controversial subject matter. Their self-titled debut album, released in 1967, is now widely regarded as one of the most influential and groundbreaking albums of all time. The Velvet Underground's legacy has had a profound impact on popular music, with their influence still felt today in countless artists and genres.

Koes Plus

Koes Plus, known for their pop and rock fusion music, also had a rebellious and hard rock side that is often overlooked. The band's albums "Dheg Dheg Plas" and "To the So Called 'the Guilties'" showcase this edgier sound, which was influenced by the emerging rock music scene of the 1960s. These albums feature heavy guitar riffs, distorted solos, and driving rhythms, showcasing Koes Plus's versatility and willingness to experiment with different genres. The band's rebellious spirit is also evident in their lyrics, which often address social and political issues, challenging the status quo and calling for change.

Albums

Albums had been the primary musical form for quite a few decades. Although they are now deemed less relevant, they're still my favourite unit of music, as in I listen to more albums than I do to individual songs. Below are some of my favourite albums ever.

The Beach Boys - Pet Sounds

Pet Sounds is a groundbreaking album released by the Beach Boys in 1966 that is widely regarded as one of the most innovative albums in the history of popular music. Produced by Brian Wilson, the album's lush orchestral arrangements, unconventional instrumentation, and complex vocal harmonies were revolutionary at the time, and have since inspired countless artists across multiple genres. The album's use of non-traditional instruments such as bicycle bells, dog whistles, and barking dogs, as well as its incorporation of unusual recording techniques helped to push the boundaries of what was possible in the recording studio. Pet Sounds is a testament to the Beach Boys' musical genius and remains a timeless classic that continues to captivate listeners to this day.

The Doors - The Doors

The Doors' self-titled debut album, released in 1967, is a testament to the band's innovativeness and experimentation. The album's unique sound, fusing Jim Morrison's poetic lyrics and intense vocal performances with Ray Manzarek's keyboard-driven melodies and Robby Krieger's bluesy guitar riffs, marked a departure from the prevailing musical styles of the time. The Doors' incorporation of elements of jazz, blues, and psychedelia, along with their use of unconventional instrumentation, such as the use of a bass keyboard in lieu of a traditional bass guitar, helped to create a sound that was truly original and groundbreaking. The album's standout tracks, including "Break On Through (To the Other Side)" and "Light My Fire," quickly became radio staples and cemented The Doors' place in rock and roll history as one of the most innovative and influential bands of all time.

Gombloh - Sekar Mayang

Sekar Mayang is a seminal album by the late Indonesian musician Gombloh backed up by the band Lemon Tree's Anno '69, released in 1981 by Golden Hand Records. The album features Gombloh's unique blend of traditional Indonesian music with elements of progressive rock and jazz, creating a sound that was truly innovative and have not since been replicated. Gombloh's lyrics, often infused with political and social commentary as found in the album, resonated with audiences across Indonesia and helped to cement his place as one of the country's most beloved musicians. Sekar Mayang is a testament to Gombloh's musical genius and remains a timeless classic in the canon of Indonesian music.

Songs

What are albums without songs? Here I will be listing some of my favourite songs of all time.

Pink Floyd - A Saucerful of Secrets

"A Saucerful of Secrets" by Pink Floyd is a hauntingly beautiful and complex composition that showcases the band's incredible musicianship and experimental sound. The song starts with a dreamy and mysterious melody, building up to a powerful and explosive climax that's both hypnotic and cathartic. The use of various musical elements such as Mellotron, distorted guitar riffs, and vocal harmonies creates a multi-layered soundscape that's both otherworldly and deeply emotional. The song is a testament to Pink Floyd's ability to create a sonic journey that takes the listener on an unforgettable ride. It's a true masterpiece of progressive rock and remains one of the band's most iconic works.

Slint - Washer

"Washer" by Slint is an emotionally intense song that's widely regarded as a masterpiece of post-rock music. The song starts with a slow and melancholic guitar riff, gradually building up to a cathartic climax that's both powerful and restrained. The use of sparse instrumentation, muted vocals, and subtle dynamics creates a sense of tension and unease that's palpable throughout the song. The lyrics are poetic and evocative, conveying a sense of longing and loss that's both personal and universal. "Washer" is a testament to Slint's unique approach to songwriting and their ability to create music that's both deeply affecting and challenging. It remains a touchstone for post-rock and a classic of the genre.

Deltron 3030 - Mastermind

"Mastermind" by Deltron 3030 is a dynamic and genre-defying song that showcases the group's innovative approach to hip-hop music. The song blends elements of electronic, funk, and rock music into a seamless and engaging whole, creating a unique sound that's both futuristic and retro at the same time. The lyrics are dense and intricate, weaving a complex narrative about a futuristic society ruled by a powerful computer AI. The use of live instrumentation and creative sampling techniques adds depth and texture to the song, creating a rich and immersive listening experience. "Mastermind" is a true masterpiece of hip-hop music, showcasing the boundless creativity and vision of Deltron 3030.
